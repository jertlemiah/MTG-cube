Curve Fitting

The basic idea of curve fitting is that it doesn't matter what you play, as long as you can keep consistently playing things every turn.

There are two main things to think about to ensure that this happens:

  1. Pick 1-2 colors and stick to them - the more colors you have, the harder it is to be consistent
  2. Fill out a decent mana-curve - you need to pick cards of varying mana values to do this

Pick 1-2 Colors

First off, don't worry about this in the first pack: be as flexible as possible early on and just pick cards that look good regardless of the color.

However by halfway through the draft you should pick a primary and/or secondary color and stick to it. My cube purposely does not contain any 3, 4, or 5 color cards to help make it easier to stick to fewer colors.

You can definitely still change your mind or splash an additional color, but you run the risk of not getting enough cards to make that strategy work. The fewer colors you have, the more consistent your deck will be because you will be able to play all your cards without getting mana-screwed.

The 40-Card Manacurve

A manacurve is just the distribution of the mana value of the cards in your deck. Generally speaking, the 40-card manacurve looks just like a normal distribution curve offset towards 2-3 mana a bit.

So it will look a bit like this:

A 40 Magic deck should roughly follow :

  • 16-18 lands
  • 22-24 non-lands
  • Twice as many creatures as non-creatures (so 15ish creatures, 7ish non-creatures)

Why Curve Fitting Works The simple answer is that I have personally designed the cube, hand-selecting every single card and trimming any cards that aren’t good enough. I have tried to make sure that cards within a color are cohesive while following the ideal mana curve and still enabling synergies that span over 2 or 3 colors.
